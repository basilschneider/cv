  \textbf{\Large Research accomplishments of Basil Schneider}

  The ATLAS experiment is a large international collaboration. Publications are always the work of a large number of people. Here I summarize the relevant
  contributions that I made in the framework of the collaboration.

  \textbf{\Large Hardware}

  \begin{itemize}

    \setlength{\itemsep}{\itemseparation}

    \item I tested optical receivers to be used for the readout of the insertable $b$-layer for reliability, frequency and input sensitivity. I defined the tests
      and performed them.

    \item To document the tests of the optical receivers, I set up a Twiki site and presented the results on different insertable $b$-layer
      general meetings.

    \item I built up the hardware activities at the University of Bern. With my efforts, the lab became known throughout the insertable $b$-layer community. The
      University of Bern is still active in this field and considered as expert in terms of optical receivers, thanks to my contributions.

  \end{itemize}

  \textbf{\Large Analysis}

  \begin{itemize}

    \setlength{\itemsep}{\itemseparation}

    \item The binned signal region used in a supersymmetry search published in JHEP is completely my idea and my work. It has also been combined with another
      search for SUSY in a final state with two leptons and was presented in the CERN courier journal.

    \item I used my expertise and gave guidance for the optimization of the signal regions with at least one tau in our latest publication.

    \item I carried out the statistical interpretation for various publications, all searching for supersymmetry in either three lepton or four lepton final
      states.

    \item For the statistical interpretation, I provided $p$-values, visible cross-sections, exclusion limit contours and upper limits on model cross-sections.

    \item I was responsible for the calculations to derive limits with asymptotic formulae or with Monte Carlo pseudo-experiments. I was also comparing results of
      these two methods to make a decision if the asymptotic formulae are good enough for our purpose.

    \item For the latest paper alone, I generated more than $2.5 \cdot 10^8$ Monte Carlo pseudo-experiments. This was done on a batch-system
      at the University of Bern with about 1500 cores. I was solely responsible for this undertaking using scripts that I partially developed.

    \item To speed up the process of determining the upper limits on the model cross-sections with Monte Carlo pseudo-experiments, I implemented a faster and more
      precise method than the one currently used as standard.

    \item To validate the results from the statistical machinery I set up and conducted many tests, e.g. how to compare the results from a multi-region fit with
      different single-region fits.

    \item For the statistical interpretation I created a RootCore package for analyzers to store their relevant events and to have a standardized framework to
      carry out the statistical interpretation. This package is used in the electroweak supersymmetry search groups with final states of two light leptons, two
      taus, three and four leptons.

    \item I collected a package of scripts of which I have written most of them myself, to carry out the statistical interpretation with the aforementioned
      RootCore package. These scripts rely on HistFitter. They are used in the electroweak supersymmetry search groups with final states of two light leptons,
      three leptons and four leptons.

    \item I made the final exclusion limit contour plots for the supersymmetry searches with a final state of three leptons.

    \item I was part of a small team setting up a RootCore package called ``Common Code'', a central framework to do the cutflow in different electroweak
      supersymmetry analyses.

    \item I set up a framework for a simultaneous fit method to determine the most important background, $WZ$, in a supersymmetry search with three leptons in the
      final state.

    \item In our latest paper, I provided most of the figures.

    \item I presented two analyses with three leptons in the final state on two different SUSY-approvals.

    \item I was selected to present posters on the supersymmetry searches in a final state with three leptons at the PLHC conference 2011 in
      Perugia and at the 1\textsuperscript{st} LHC Physics conference 2013 in Barcelona.

  \end{itemize}
